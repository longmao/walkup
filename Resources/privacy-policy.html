Walk Up — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026

Walk Up is a free iPhone alarm app that requires you to walk a number of steps to dismiss the alarm. This policy describes what data the app handles, in plain language.

The short version

Walk Up does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. The app does not require an account. The app contains no advertising, no tracking SDKs, and no third-party analytics. The only data the app touches is your step count, and only while the alarm is actively ringing on your device.

Step counting

Walk Up uses Apple's CoreMotion framework (specifically CMPedometer) to count steps on your iPhone. This happens locally on your device. Step counts are:

  • Read only during the time an alarm is ringing.
  • Used only to determine when the required step count has been reached.
  • Never stored to disk, never uploaded to any server, never shared with any third party.

Motion permission

The first time you set or test an alarm, iOS will ask you to allow motion access. The app uses this permission solely to count steps during alarm ringing. If you decline, you can still dismiss the alarm by long-pressing the on-screen button for 3 seconds. We do not request motion access for any other purpose.

Notifications

Walk Up uses local notifications (delivered by iOS itself, not push notifications from a server) to fire alarms. The app requests notification permission when you enable your first alarm. You can revoke this at any time in iOS Settings.

Data not collected

Walk Up does not collect:

  • Name, email, or any contact info
  • Location (GPS or network)
  • Health data from Apple Health / HealthKit
  • Device identifiers for tracking
  • Usage analytics or crash reports sent to a server
  • Any data entered by you (no notes, no recordings, no journaling)

No accounts, no servers

Walk Up has no user accounts and no backend servers. There is nothing to log into, nothing to sync, nothing to back up. If you delete the app, all data (the single alarm config) is removed from your device.

Children's privacy

Walk Up does not target children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any data from children. The app contains no advertising or third-party content that would be inappropriate for any age.

Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, we will change the "Last updated" date above and, if the changes are material, surface a notice in the app's next release. The current version of the app will always link to this page from the About tab.
